# Excavation and Groundworks

## Permit to Dig

No excavation, including hand digging, post holes, and soil nails, may start without a permit to dig issued by the section engineer. The permit confirms that buried services drawings have been reviewed, that a cable avoidance tool (CAT) and signal generator survey has been completed and marked on the ground, and that trial holes have been dug by hand where services are indicated. Permits are valid for the area, depth, and duration stated on them and must be re-validated if conditions change.

Mechanical excavation is not permitted within 500 mm of a marked service; the final approach to any service must be by hand digging with insulated tools or by vacuum excavation. Any service found that is not on the drawings must be treated as live, work stopped, and the section engineer informed.

## Trench Support and Battering

The decision on how to support an excavation rests with the temporary works coordinator and depends on soil type, depth, groundwater, surcharge from plant and spoil, and the duration the excavation will stay open. No one may enter an unsupported excavation deeper than 1.2 metres on this project unless the sides are battered back to a safe angle or a written assessment by the temporary works coordinator justifies it.

Support options include:

- Battering the sides to a stable angle, typically no steeper than the natural angle of repose of the soil.
- Trench boxes and drag boxes, installed and moved so that workers are never in an unsupported length.
- Proprietary hydraulic shoring, installed from outside the trench wherever the system allows.
- Sheet piling for deep, long-duration, or waterlogged excavations, designed by the temporary works designer.

Excavations must be inspected by a competent person before each shift, after rain, after any collapse or near miss, and after any change in loading nearby. Inspection results are recorded in the excavation register.

## Spoil, Materials, and Plant Near Edges

Spoil heaps and stored materials must be set back from the excavation edge by at least the depth of the excavation, and in no case closer than 1 metre, so that the surcharge does not destabilise the sides and material cannot roll into the trench. Vehicle stop blocks must be positioned where tipping or delivery vehicles approach an edge. Excavators must slew and travel so that tracks stay outside the zone of influence defined by the temporary works coordinator.

## Access, Egress, and Edge Protection

Safe access into any excavation deeper than 1 metre must be by secured ladder, with a ladder positioned so that no worker is ever more than 15 metres from a means of escape along the trench. Excavations in or near pedestrian areas must be protected by fixed barriers, not tape alone, and lit during darkness. Crossing points over trenches must be purpose-made walkways with guardrails on both sides.

## Groundwater and Confined Space Risks

Where groundwater is present, the dewatering method must be agreed with the temporary works coordinator, and pumps must discharge through settlement tanks before water leaves the site. Rising water in an excavation is an evacuation trigger, not a nuisance: leave the excavation and report it.

Deep excavations, manholes, and chambers can accumulate hazardous atmospheres and may be confined spaces. Entry into any chamber or shaft requires atmosphere testing before entry and continuous monitoring, a top person in constant communication, and rescue equipment at the point of entry. Petrol or diesel plant must never run inside or at the edge of a confined excavation where exhaust can accumulate.

## Backfilling and Reinstatement

Backfill must be placed in compacted layers to the specification for the location, with support systems withdrawn progressively as filling proceeds, never removed entirely in advance of the fill. Marker tape must be laid above reinstated services at the depths given in the services specification, and the as-built position of any new or exposed service must be recorded and passed to the section engineer for the site services drawings.
