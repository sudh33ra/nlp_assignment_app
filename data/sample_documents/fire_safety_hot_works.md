# Fire Safety and Hot Works

## Fire Risk on Construction Sites

Construction sites present elevated fire risk because temporary buildings, stored materials, and hot work coincide with incomplete fire compartments and detection systems. The site fire plan, displayed in the site office and at each fire point, shows escape routes, fire point locations, assembly points, and the location of the temporary fire alarm call points. The fire plan is updated whenever site layout changes affect escape routes.

## Hot Work Permits

Hot work is any work involving flame, sparks, or heat sources capable of igniting materials: welding, flame cutting, grinding, soldering, bitumen boilers, and heat guns. All hot work outside the designated hot work bay requires a hot work permit issued by the site supervisor for that shift only.

The permit process requires:

1. Removal of combustible materials within 10 metres of the work, or protection with fire blankets where removal is impracticable.
2. A suitable fire extinguisher at the workface, checked and in date, with the operative trained to use it.
3. Screening of sparks with fire-retardant sheeting where other trades or the public are nearby.
4. A fire watch maintained during the work and for at least 60 minutes after the last hot work of the shift, with the watch period recorded on the permit before it is closed.
5. Hot work must stop at least one hour before the site closes so the fire watch can be completed.

Permits must be returned to the site office and closed at the end of the task. An open permit found after the shift is treated as a near miss and investigated.

## Extinguishers and Fire Points

Fire points are positioned so that no workface is more than 30 metres from the nearest point. Each fire point carries a rotary alarm or air horn, signage, and extinguishers appropriate to the surrounding risk:

- Water or foam extinguishers for general combustible materials such as timber and packaging.
- Carbon dioxide extinguishers for electrical equipment and live panels.
- Dry powder extinguishers near fuel storage and plant refuelling areas.

Extinguishers must not be removed from fire points to the workface except under a hot work permit, and must be returned or replaced immediately after use. Discharged or damaged extinguishers must be reported to the site office the same day. Fire points are inspected weekly and the inspection recorded.

Only attempt to fight a fire if it is smaller than a waste-paper bin, you have a clear escape route behind you, and the alarm has already been raised. Otherwise evacuate immediately.

## Storage of Flammable Materials

Flammable liquids, gas cylinders, and other highly combustible materials must be stored in the designated external compounds, never inside the building under construction.

- Store flammable liquids in a ventilated, bunded, lockable store at least 10 metres from site accommodation and boundaries. Keep quantities at the workface to the minimum needed for the shift, in approved safety containers.
- Store gas cylinders upright, secured against falling, in a caged external compound with full and empty cylinders segregated. Close cylinder valves when not in use and at the end of every shift.
- Keep oxygen cylinders at least 3 metres from fuel gas cylinders in storage, or separated by a fire-resistant partition.
- Site LPG heaters must be fed from cylinders located outside the space being heated, with hoses inspected daily.

Waste that can self-heat, such as oily rags and used sealant cloths, must be placed in closed metal bins and removed from the building daily.

## Temporary Accommodation and Escape Routes

Site cabins must be separated from the building under construction, from each other where practicable, and from flammable stores by the distances given in the fire plan. Escape routes within the building must be kept clear, lit where natural light is inadequate, and signed with photoluminescent signage as the structure progresses. Fire doors in completed sections must not be wedged open. Smoking is permitted only in the designated smoking shelter.
