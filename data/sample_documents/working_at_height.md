# Working at Height

## General Requirements

Work at height means work in any place where a person could fall a distance liable to cause personal injury. This includes work on ladders, scaffolds, roofs, and near excavations or floor openings. Work at height must be avoided where reasonably practicable; where it cannot be avoided, work must be planned, supervised, and carried out using equipment that prevents falls or minimises the distance and consequences of a fall.

A working-at-height plan must be prepared before any task above 2 metres, and for any task below 2 metres where a fall could still cause injury, such as work adjacent to protruding rebar or an open excavation.

## Ladders and Stepladders

Ladders may only be used for short-duration light work, generally up to 30 minutes at a position, where a risk assessment shows that more suitable equipment is not justified.

- Inspect ladders before each use for damaged stiles, rungs, and feet. Tag and remove damaged ladders from service.
- Set leaning ladders at a 75-degree angle, approximately one unit out for every four units up.
- Secure the ladder at the top, or where this is not possible, at the base; footing by a second person is a last resort.
- Extend the ladder at least 1 metre above the landing point where it is used for access.
- Maintain three points of contact when climbing. Do not carry heavy or bulky loads up a ladder; use a tool belt or hoist line.
- Do not stand on the top three rungs of a leaning ladder or the top two steps of a stepladder.

Class 1 industrial or EN 131 professional ladders are the only classes permitted on site. Domestic-class ladders must not be brought onto site.

## Scaffolding

Scaffolds must be erected, altered, and dismantled only by trained and competent scaffolders under a handover procedure. Incomplete scaffolds must display a prominent "Scaffold Incomplete – Do Not Use" sign.

Every working platform must have:

- Double guardrails, with the top rail at least 950 mm above the platform and no gap exceeding 470 mm.
- Toe boards at least 150 mm high on all open edges.
- Fully boarded decks with boards secured against uplift and displacement.
- Safe ladder access within the scaffold bay, with the ladder gate or hatch kept closed when not in use.

Scaffolds must be inspected by a competent person before first use, after any alteration or adverse weather, and at intervals not exceeding seven days. Inspection records are kept in the scaffold register in the site office. Never remove ties, boards, or guardrails; request an alteration through the scaffolding contractor.

## Mobile Towers

Mobile access towers must be assembled by operatives holding current tower training, following the manufacturer's instruction manual. Wheels must be locked before use, and the tower must never be moved with people or materials on the platform. The maximum platform height must not exceed the manufacturer's stated ratio to base dimensions, or the tower must be tied or ballasted.

## Edge Protection and Openings

All floor edges, roof edges, and openings through which a person could fall must be protected with fixed edge protection: double guardrails and toe boards, or full covers secured against displacement and marked "Opening – Do Not Remove".

Where fixed protection is not practicable for short-duration work, a personal fall protection system may be used. Fall restraint, which prevents the user from reaching the edge, is preferred over fall arrest. Anyone using a harness must be trained, and harnesses must be inspected before each use and formally examined every six months. A rescue plan must be in place before any fall-arrest work begins; suspension in a harness after a fall can cause serious harm within minutes.

## Fragile Surfaces

Fragile roof surfaces, including rooflights and fibre cement sheets, must be identified before roof work starts. Work on or near fragile surfaces requires crawling boards or staging that spans the supports, together with edge protection or fall arrest. Warning signs must be fixed at every access point to a fragile roof.
