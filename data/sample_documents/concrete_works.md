# Concrete Works

## Planning and Sequencing

All concrete pours must be planned in advance and recorded on the pour card system. The pour card records the element to be cast, the concrete mix reference, the planned volume, and the required checks before, during, and after the pour. No pour may begin until the pour card has been signed by the section engineer confirming that formwork, reinforcement, and embedded items have been checked against the drawings.

Concrete deliveries must be scheduled so that each load is placed within its workability window. As a general rule on this project, concrete must be discharged within 90 minutes of batching unless the mix design includes a retarder and the supplier confirms an extended window on the delivery ticket.

## Formwork

Formwork must be designed for the full pressure of fresh concrete, including the effects of pour rate, concrete temperature, and vibration. The temporary works coordinator must approve all formwork designs and any departures from them.

- Erect formwork on firm, level bearings; check props for plumb and correct pitch.
- Seal joints to prevent grout loss, which weakens edges and creates honeycombing.
- Apply release agent thinly and evenly before fixing reinforcement, not after.
- Provide safe access platforms for placing and vibrating concrete; do not climb on formwork.

Formwork must be inspected and signed off immediately before the pour. Striking times must follow the striking schedule, which is based on concrete strength gain rather than fixed ages; in cold weather, striking must be delayed until maturity calculations or cube results confirm adequate strength. Props supporting soffits must not be removed early to recover materials.

## Reinforcement

Reinforcement must be fixed as shown on the bending schedules and placement drawings, with the correct cover maintained using purpose-made spacers. Cover blocks made from broken concrete or timber offcuts are not permitted. Projecting starter bars must be protected with proprietary caps to prevent impalement injuries wherever a person could fall onto them.

## Placing and Compaction

Concrete must be placed as close as possible to its final position and must not be moved laterally by vibration, which causes segregation. The maximum free-fall height for concrete is 1.5 metres; use chutes, tremie pipes, or pump lines for deeper drops.

Compact concrete with internal pokers in layers not exceeding 500 mm. Insert the poker vertically at regular spacings, penetrate the layer below by about 100 mm to knit the layers together, and withdraw it slowly. Over-vibration brings excess fines and water to the surface; under-vibration leaves honeycombing around reinforcement and in corners.

Workers handling fresh concrete must wear waterproof gloves, long sleeves, and waterproof boots. Wet cement is corrosive to skin and can cause serious chemical burns; any skin contact must be washed off immediately with clean water, and contaminated clothing changed.

## Curing

Curing protects concrete from early drying and temperature extremes so that it gains its design strength. Begin curing as soon as the surface can accept it without damage.

- For slabs, apply curing compound in an even coat, or cover with polythene sheeting lapped and weighted at the edges.
- Keep surfaces continuously damp where water curing is specified; intermittent wetting causes surface crazing.
- Maintain curing for at least seven days for ordinary structural mixes, or as specified for special mixes.

## Cold Weather Concreting

When the air temperature is at or below 5 degrees Celsius and falling, cold weather precautions apply. Concrete must not be placed against frozen ground, ice, or frost-covered reinforcement. The temperature of the concrete at delivery must be at least 5 degrees Celsius, and the placed concrete must be kept above 5 degrees Celsius until it reaches 5 N/mm² strength, using insulated blankets, enclosures, or heated aggregates as necessary. Accelerating admixtures may be used only with the approval of the section engineer, and calcium chloride is not permitted in reinforced concrete.
