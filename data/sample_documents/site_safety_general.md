# General Site Safety Guidance

## Purpose and Scope

This document provides general safety guidance for personnel working on construction sites managed under the Riverside Development programme. It covers personal protective equipment, site access, housekeeping, and communication of hazards. It applies to all workers, visitors, and delivery drivers entering the site boundary.

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

All personnel entering the working area must wear the mandatory minimum PPE at all times:

- Safety helmet with chin strap in good condition, replaced at least every three years or after any significant impact.
- High-visibility vest or jacket, kept clean enough to remain conspicuous.
- Safety boots with steel or composite toe caps and midsole protection.

Additional task-specific PPE is required for certain activities. Eye protection must be worn when cutting, grinding, or drilling. Hearing protection is required in designated high-noise zones, indicated by blue signage, and whenever plant operates within 5 metres. Gloves appropriate to the task must be selected using the glove selection chart displayed in the site office.

Damaged PPE must be reported to the site supervisor and exchanged before work continues. PPE must not be modified or painted, as this can conceal damage or reduce protective performance.

## Site Access and Sign-In

All personnel must sign in at the site office before starting work and sign out when leaving. The sign-in record is used for roll call in an emergency, so it must be accurate at all times.

First-time visitors must complete the site induction before unescorted access is permitted. The induction covers emergency arrangements, traffic routes, welfare facilities, and permit requirements. Inductions are valid for twelve months on this site.

Pedestrian routes are separated from vehicle routes by physical barriers wherever practicable. Where routes cross, marked crossing points with mirrors or banksman control are provided. Never walk behind reversing plant. Make eye contact with the operator before approaching any machine.

## Housekeeping

Good housekeeping is the single most effective control against slips, trips, and falls on this site.

- Keep walkways and stairs clear of materials, cables, and debris at all times.
- Store materials in designated laydown areas, stacked stably and no higher than shoulder height unless racked.
- Route temporary cables overhead or through protective ramps, never loose across walkways.
- Dispose of waste in the segregated skips provided; do not allow waste to accumulate at the workface.
- Clean up spills immediately and use warning signs until the area is dry.

Each trade is responsible for clearing its own work area at the end of every shift. The site supervisor conducts a daily housekeeping walk and records findings in the site diary.

## Hazard Reporting

Any worker who identifies a hazard must make the area safe if it is safe to do so, warn others nearby, and report the hazard to the site supervisor without delay. Hazard report cards are available at the site office and can be submitted anonymously.

Near misses must be reported in the same way as accidents. Near-miss data is reviewed weekly to identify trends before they result in injury.

## Emergency Arrangements

The emergency assembly point is the car park adjacent to the main gate. On hearing the continuous alarm, stop work, isolate equipment if safe to do so, and walk to the assembly point by the nearest safe route. Do not use hoists during an evacuation. The fire marshal takes the roll call using the sign-in record.

First aid boxes are located in the site office and the ground-floor welfare unit. The names of trained first aiders are displayed on the safety noticeboard.
