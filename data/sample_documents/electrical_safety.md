# Electrical Safety on Site

## Temporary Site Electrics

Temporary electrical installations on this site are designed, installed, and maintained by the appointed electrical contractor. No other trade may alter distribution boards, transformers, or fixed wiring. Requests for additional supplies must be made through the site office.

Portable tools on site operate at 110 volts centre-tapped-to-earth, supplied through yellow transformers and connectors, so that the maximum voltage to earth is limited to 55 volts. Battery-powered tools are preferred wherever practicable and are mandatory in damp or confined locations unless a specific risk assessment approves otherwise. Mains-voltage 230-volt equipment is not permitted in the general working area; it may be used only in the site offices and welfare units, protected by 30 mA RCDs.

Generators must be positioned in ventilated outdoor locations, earthed in accordance with the electrical contractor's arrangements, and refuelled only when shut down and cool.

## Cable Management

Trailing cables are a leading cause of trips and of damage-related electrical faults on construction sites.

- Route cables at high level on proprietary hooks wherever possible, keeping them clear of walkways, doorways, and vehicle routes.
- Where cables must cross a walkway or roadway at ground level, protect them with cable ramps rated for the traffic expected.
- Keep connectors out of standing water and off wet ground; suspend them or place them on stands.
- Uncoil extension leads fully before use to prevent overheating.
- Inspect cables daily for cuts, abrasion, and damaged plugs, and remove damaged items from service immediately.

Buried services must be located, marked, and where necessary exposed by hand digging before any excavation begins; see the excavation guidance document for the full permit-to-dig procedure.

## Inspection and Testing Intervals

All portable and transportable electrical equipment used on site must carry a current test label. The combined inspection and test intervals for this project are:

- 110-volt portable tools and leads: every 3 months.
- 230-volt equipment in site offices: every 12 months.
- RCDs: user check with the test button before each use, plus formal test every 3 months.
- Fixed temporary installation: inspected and tested every 3 months by the electrical contractor.

Equipment without a valid test label, or with an expired label, must be taken out of use and returned to the tool store. User checks before each use remain essential regardless of the test label: look for damaged casings, bent pins, loose cable grips, and signs of overheating.

## Lockout and Isolation

Work on any electrical circuit or on plant with stored energy requires isolation under the site lockout-tagout procedure.

1. Identify all energy sources feeding the equipment, including secondary supplies and control circuits.
2. Isolate at the identified points and lock each isolator with a personal padlock; every worker on the task fits their own lock to the multi-lock hasp.
3. Fit a tag stating the work in progress, the name of the person who applied the lock, and the date.
4. Prove the circuit dead at the point of work with a voltage indicator, proving the indicator on a known live source or proving unit before and after.
5. Only the person who fitted a lock may remove it. Locks may be removed by the site manager only under the documented emergency lock-removal procedure.

Live working is not permitted on this site. Fault-finding that requires energised equipment must be referred to the electrical contractor and carried out under their safe system of work.

## Overhead Lines and Plant

Where overhead power lines cross or border the site, goalposts and barriers define the safe passage routes and exclusion zones. Cranes, excavators, tipper bodies, and ladders must never enter the exclusion zone. If any plant or load contacts an overhead line, the operator must stay in the cab, warn others to keep clear, and contact the site office; leave the cab by jumping clear, without touching the machine and the ground at the same time, only if fire makes staying impossible.
